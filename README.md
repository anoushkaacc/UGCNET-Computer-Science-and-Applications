# About UGC NET

The **University Grants Commission - National Eligibility Test (UGC NET)** is a national-level examination conducted by the **National Testing Agency (NTA)** on behalf of the **University Grants Commission (UGC)**.

The examination determines the eligibility of candidates for:

- Assistant Professor
- Junior Research Fellowship (JRF)
- Admission to Ph.D. programmes (as per current UGC regulations)

The examination is conducted twice a year across multiple subjects, with **Computer Science and Applications (Subject Code: 87)** being one of the technical disciplines.

---

# Why Take UGC NET?

Qualifying UGC NET can provide opportunities such as:

- Eligibility for Assistant Professor positions in universities and colleges.
- Eligibility for Junior Research Fellowship (JRF), which offers financial support for pursuing research.
- Admission to Ph.D. programmes in many higher education institutions.
- Improved academic and research career prospects.

---

# Examination Pattern

The examination consists of **two papers**, both conducted in a **single session**.

| Paper | Questions | Marks | Duration |
|--------|----------:|------:|---------:|
| Paper I | 50 | 100 | Included in the 3-hour session |
| Paper II | 100 | 200 | Included in the 3-hour session |

**Total**

- 150 Questions
- 300 Marks
- 3 Hours
- Computer-Based Test (CBT)
- Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

There is **no negative marking** for incorrect answers.

---

# Marking Scheme

| Response | Marks |
|----------|------:|
| Correct Answer | +2 |
| Incorrect Answer | 0 |
| Unattempted | 0 |

Every question carries equal weight.

---

# Qualifying Criteria

The qualifying criteria are determined by the National Testing Agency based on category-wise cut-offs and overall performance. Meeting the minimum qualifying marks alone does not guarantee qualification, as the final result depends on the official cut-off for the examination cycle.
