## About UGC NET

The **University Grants Commission - National Eligibility Test (UGC NET)** is a national-level examination conducted by the **National Testing Agency (NTA)** on behalf of the **University Grants Commission (UGC)**.

The examination determines the eligibility of candidates for:

* Assistant Professor
* Junior Research Fellowship (JRF)
* Admission to Ph.D. programmes (as per current UGC regulations)

It is conducted twice a year across multiple subjects, with **Computer Science and Applications (Subject Code: 87)** being one of the technical disciplines.

---

## Why Take UGC NET?

Qualifying UGC NET provides opportunities for:

* Assistant Professor positions in universities and colleges
* Junior Research Fellowship (JRF)
* Admission to Ph.D. programmes
* Academic and research careers

## Qualifying Criteria

The qualifying criteria are determined by the National Testing Agency (NTA) based on category-wise cut-offs and overall candidate performance. Meeting the minimum qualifying marks alone does not guarantee qualification, as the final result depends on the official cut-off for the respective examination cycle.
